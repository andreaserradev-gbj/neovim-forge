## Copilot on the Corne

Copilot operations are simple:

| Operation | Keys | Hand |
|---|---|---|
| Accept | Tab | Right thumb or left outer |
| Dismiss | Escape | J+K or configured |
| Cycle | `<C-]>` | iCtrl + ] |

All comfortable. The Corne's advantage: you're not reaching for awkward key combinations. Tab is accessible, Escape is home row, Ctrl is the inner key.

---

## The Keyboard-First Advantage

Copilot is most useful when you can:
1. Read the suggestion (1-2 seconds)
2. Accept or dismiss (1 keystroke)
3. Continue editing

This tight feedback loop only works on a keyboard. With a mouse, you lose the rhythm.

The Corne makes this workflow smooth because every operation is one or two keystrokes from home position.

---

## Workflow Example

1. Type: `function processData(data)`
2. Copilot suggests the body
3. Tab to accept (right thumb)
4. Review (glance)
5. `u` to undo if wrong
6. Or `dif` to replace the body
7. Continue

Zero context switching. Pure keyboard flow.
