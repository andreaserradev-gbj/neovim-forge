## The Philosophy in Practice

Vim isn't about memorizing commands. It's about understanding composition:

- **Motions** describe distance (w, j, %)
- **Operators** describe action (d, c, y)
- **Count** describes repetition (5, 3)
- **Text objects** describe semantic units (iw, af, ip)

Combine them: `d3w` = delete 3 words. `caf` = change around function. `y2ip` = yank 2 paragraphs.

The grammar is consistent. Once you understand it, you can compose operations that don't exist as single commands. You're fluent, not reading from a cheatsheet.
