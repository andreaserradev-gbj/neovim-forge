## The Complete Workflow on the Corne

By Day 28, every operation is a short sequence of keystrokes:

- **Navigate:** Space + f + f (find files), or Space + s + g (grep)
- **Mark:** m + letter (local), m + Shift+letter (global)
- **Fold:** z + a/c/o/R/M
- **Edit:** operator + i/a + letter (text objects)
- **Replace:** Space + s + r (grug-far)
- **Terminal:** Space + f + t
- **Commit:** Command-line in terminal

All of these fit naturally on the 46-key Corne. Your hands stay near home row. Context-switching is minimal.

This is where the Corne + Vim philosophy fully realizes itself: the keyboard becomes transparent. You're not thinking about keys; you're thinking about your code.

## Speed Gains from a Split Keyboard

By Week 4, you've learned that split keyboards + Vim compound in speed:
- Each hand operates independently
- Modifiers are accessible without stretching
- The columnar layout makes motions and text objects natural
- No reaching to the top-left for Escape
- No reaching to reach corners for rarely-used keys

A developer who started with a standard keyboard and Vim might plateau around 20-30% faster than mouse-based editing. A developer using Corne + Vim can reach 2-3x faster because the entire system is optimized for minimal hand movement.
